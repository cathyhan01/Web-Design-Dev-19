About Mason Bates

His background and influences

Mason Bates, a Grammy award-winning American composer

Mason Bates is a American symphonic composer from Virginia famous for championing the integration of unique electronic sounds into his performances. He earned degrees in both English literature and music composition from the Columbia University-Julliard skill, and subsequently graduated from UC Berkeley with a PhD in composition in 2008. Although classically trained, his early career features him as a DJ in various San Francisco and Oakland nightclubs and lounges, continuing on to this day as ‘DJ Masonic’. Both DJing and electronic performing were known to be a major influence on Bates’ musical style, which focuses on the integration of electronic sounds within an orchestra. His pushes the boundaries of what can be considered classical music, whereas his contemporary style presents music in new formats, bringing classical music to new audiences.

Mimicking the artistry of Mason Bates

Mason Bates uses his computer in his performance, treating it as any other instrument. It’s used to synthesize the electronic, machine-like sounds heard in Mothership. A synth pad is a grid-like device that plays multiple sounds that may or may not be included in the orchestral instrumentation.
